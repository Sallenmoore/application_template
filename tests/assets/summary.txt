In the absurd theater of life, a dog found itself impaled on a garden fork, frozen in a pose reminiscent of a dream-chase. The canine's demise, likely due to the fork, was a spectacle on Mrs Shears' lawn, a grim tableau at midnight. The narrator, in a display of morbid curiosity, ruled out other causes of death like cancer or a road accident, because who in their right mind would stick a fork into an already dead dog? But then again, certainty is a luxury in this nonsensical world.
